## Temperature Trend Analysis

Using ERA5 monthly mean 2-meter temperature data for the Carpathian Basin (1980–2025), annual mean temperatures were calculated from spatially averaged monthly values.

Results:

* Total warming (1980–2025): 2.93°C
* Linear warming trend: 0.557°C per decade

The observed warming is substantial and suggests that any future cooling influence associated with AMOC weakening would need to be evaluated relative to a strong existing warming trend.

A continentality index was defined as:

C = Summer Temperature − Winter Temperature

Results:

- Continentality trend: -0.049 °C per decade

### Preliminary Interpretation

The observational record does not indicate increasing continentality in terms of seasonal temperature contrast. Instead, strong warming occurred throughout the year, with winter warming slightly exceeding summer warming.

The Carpathian Basin nevertheless experienced substantially faster warming than Western Europe, supporting the hypothesis that inland Central Europe responds differently from Atlantic-influenced regions.

Future work will investigate precipitation trends, drought indicators, and the potential role of AMOC variability in shaping regional climate responses.
