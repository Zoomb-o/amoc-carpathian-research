# Project Summary

## Research Question

Has the Carpathian Basin warmed faster than Western Europe, and is this enhanced warming related to Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC) variability?

## Main Findings

### Long-Term Warming (1980–2025)

Carpathian Basin:
+0.557 °C per decade

Western Europe:
+0.304 °C per decade

Difference:
+0.252 °C per decade

### RAPID Observation Period (2004–2024)

Carpathian Basin:
+0.895 °C per decade

Western Europe:
+0.338 °C per decade

Difference:
+0.557 °C per decade

Warming Ratio:
2.65

### AMOC

Mean:
16.98 Sv

Trend:
-0.951 Sv per decade

### Correlations

AMOC vs Summer Temperature:

r = -0.583
p = 0.0055

AMOC vs Temperature Difference:

r = -0.587
p = 0.0052

Permutation Test:

p = 0.0069

## Interpretation

The Carpathian Basin warmed substantially faster than Western Europe, particularly during 2004–2024. Weaker AMOC conditions were associated with both warmer Carpathian summers and larger regional temperature contrasts.

The results suggest that AMOC variability may contribute to enhanced warming in the Carpathian Basin, although the observational analysis does not establish causality.
