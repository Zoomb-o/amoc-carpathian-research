# Figure Captions

## Figure 1

Annual mean temperatures in the Carpathian Basin and Western Europe derived from ERA5 reanalysis data for 1980–2025. Linear trend lines indicate stronger warming in the Carpathian Basin.

## Figure 2

Annual mean AMOC strength derived from RAPID observations at 26.5°N during 2004–2024. The fitted trend line indicates a weakening of approximately 0.951 Sv per decade.

## Figure 3

Relationship between annual mean AMOC strength and Carpathian Basin summer temperature during 2004–2024. The negative correlation indicates warmer summers during weaker AMOC conditions (r = -0.583, p = 0.0055).

## Figure 4

Relationship between annual mean AMOC strength and the temperature difference between the Carpathian Basin and Western Europe during 2004–2024. Weaker AMOC conditions are associated with enhanced relative warming in the Carpathian Basin (r = -0.587, p = 0.0052).

## Figure 5

Comparison of annual AMOC strength and Carpathian Basin temperature anomalies during 2004–2024. The figure illustrates concurrent AMOC weakening and regional warming.
