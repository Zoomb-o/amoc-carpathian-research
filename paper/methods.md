# Data and Methods

## Study Region

The primary study region is the Carpathian Basin, defined using ERA5 reanalysis grid cells covering approximately:

Latitude:
44°N–49°N

Longitude:
15°E–27°E

A comparison region representing Western Europe was also analyzed.

## Temperature Data

Monthly mean 2-metre air temperature data were obtained from the ERA5 reanalysis dataset for the period 1980–2025.

Spatial averages were calculated across all grid cells within each study region.

Temperatures were converted from Kelvin to degrees Celsius.

Annual mean temperatures were calculated by averaging monthly values within each year.

Summer temperatures were calculated using June, July, and August averages.

Winter temperatures were calculated using December, January, and February averages.

## Precipitation Data

Monthly total precipitation data were obtained from ERA5 for 1980–2025.

Annual precipitation totals were calculated from monthly values.

Summer precipitation was calculated using June–August totals.

## Mean Sea Level Pressure

Monthly mean sea level pressure (MSLP) data were obtained from ERA5 for 1980–2025.

Pressure values were converted from Pascals to hectopascals (hPa).

Annual and summer means were calculated for trend analysis.

## AMOC Data

AMOC observations were obtained from the RAPID monitoring array at 26.5°N in the Atlantic Ocean.

The dataset covers the period April 2004–March 2024.

Annual mean AMOC strength was calculated from the RAPID transport estimates.

AMOC strength is expressed in Sverdrups (Sv), where:

1 Sv = 10^6 m³ s⁻¹

## Trend Analysis

Linear trends were estimated using ordinary least-squares regression.

Reported trends are expressed per decade.

## Correlation Analysis

Pearson correlation coefficients were calculated between:

- AMOC strength and Carpathian Basin summer temperature
- AMOC strength and winter temperature
- AMOC strength and the Carpathian Basin–Western Europe temperature difference
- Pressure and temperature variables

Statistical significance was evaluated using two-sided p-values.

## Permutation Testing

A Monte Carlo permutation test was performed using 10,000 random shuffles of the temperature series.

The observed correlation was compared with the distribution of correlations obtained from randomized data.

The permutation p-value was calculated as the fraction of randomized correlations equal to or greater than the observed correlation magnitude.
