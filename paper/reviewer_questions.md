# Potential Reviewer Questions

## 1. Is a 21-year AMOC record long enough?

Response:

The RAPID observational record is limited to 2004–2024. This limitation is explicitly acknowledged in the manuscript. To address concerns regarding sample size, multiple statistical approaches were applied, including significance testing, detrended correlations, lag analysis, and a 10,000-iteration permutation test.

---

## 2. Does correlation imply causation?

Response:

No. The manuscript does not claim causality. The results indicate statistically significant associations that are consistent with a potential physical connection between AMOC variability and regional climate variability.

---

## 3. Could both AMOC and temperature be responding to a common trend?

Response:

A detrended analysis was performed. Although the correlation weakened after trend removal (r = -0.347), the relationship remained negative, suggesting that the observed association is not solely due to shared long-term trends.

---

## 4. Why use ERA5 rather than station observations?

Response:

ERA5 provides spatially complete and internally consistent coverage across the entire study region over several decades, making it suitable for regional-scale climate analysis.

---

## 5. Why compare against Western Europe?

Response:

Western Europe serves as a reference region strongly influenced by the North Atlantic climate system. Comparing the Carpathian Basin against Western Europe allows assessment of whether regional warming exceeds broader European trends.

---

## 6. What is the strongest result?

Response:

The Carpathian Basin warmed approximately 2.65 times faster than Western Europe during 2004–2024, while weaker AMOC conditions were associated with both warmer Carpathian summers and larger Carpathian–Western Europe temperature differences.
