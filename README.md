# AMOC–Carpathian Research

Investigation of long-term warming in the Carpathian Basin and its relationship with Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC) variability.

## Research Question

Has the Carpathian Basin warmed faster than Western Europe, and is this enhanced warming related to variability in the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC)?

## Main Findings

### Long-Term Warming (1980–2025)

| Region           | Trend            |
| ---------------- | ---------------- |
| Carpathian Basin | +0.557 °C/decade |
| Western Europe   | +0.304 °C/decade |

### RAPID Observation Period (2004–2024)

| Region           | Trend            |
| ---------------- | ---------------- |
| Carpathian Basin | +0.895 °C/decade |
| Western Europe   | +0.338 °C/decade |

The Carpathian Basin warmed approximately **2.65 times faster** than Western Europe during 2004–2024.

### AMOC

| Metric             | Value            |
| ------------------ | ---------------- |
| Mean AMOC Strength | 16.98 Sv         |
| Trend              | -0.951 Sv/decade |

### Correlations

AMOC vs Carpathian Basin Summer Temperature:

* r = -0.583
* p = 0.0055

AMOC vs Carpathian–Western Europe Temperature Difference:

* r = -0.587
* p = 0.0052

Permutation Test:

* p = 0.0069

## Key Conclusion

The Carpathian Basin experienced substantially stronger warming than Western Europe during the RAPID observation period. Significant negative correlations between AMOC strength and regional temperature metrics suggest that AMOC variability may contribute to enhanced warming in the Carpathian Basin, although the observational analysis does not establish causality.
